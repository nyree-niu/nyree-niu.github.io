It is very difficult to juggle between different commitments in life. I can tell where my limit is, and how much effort I can put on this learning commitment. My priority is always family comes first, then work is forced to be the second, then learning comes the last. At the end of each bootcamp unit, there is a self-assessment guide. It is always the time management, and self-displine on stick to schedule. Also exploring the best way of learning coding. I feel like I don't even have time looking after my well-being, there is endless life surprises. (fuel price increase, blocked drain, learning new tasks at work while training offshore team, school commitments)

4. How might you overcome some of these barriers as you proceed through the rest of your time in bootcamp? This should include:

When working as part of a team, I understand that every contribution is important to the overall process. I will commit to following the project timeline and make sure I provide regular updates on my progress. If I encounter any roadblocks, I will communicate them in a timely manner so the team can adjust and support each other as needed. I will approach the work as if I were contributing to a real client project, which will help me stay accountable and professional.
