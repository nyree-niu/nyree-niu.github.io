Bootcamp Reflection

April 2026

Reflection on Learning Plan

1. How accurate were your foundations expectations of your learning needs and growth areas?

I would say it was 99% accurate, which is learning full circle of creating a website, and effectively work with team.

2. Were there any surprises / did you learn new things about yourself?

Juggling all these different commitments is a massive challenge, but it is helped me figure out exactly where my limit is. I cannot do everything well at the same time. My priority list is locked in: my family, work (milk and bread), and study. To take the pressure off myself, I have used the self-assessments at the end of each Bootcamp unit to adjust my goals. Instead of stressing about hitting Level 3 (coding completely from memory), I have lowered my target to Level 2 (building with support) or Level 1 (just being able to read and understand it).

3. What challenges/barriers did you find in following your learning and wellbeing plan?

The biggest hurdle is definitely time management and keeping myself disciplined when the schedule slips. Between trying to find the best way to learn code and dealing with endless life surprises: blocked drains, rising fuel costs, school commitments, and a hectic work environment. My wellbeing plan often gets pushed. There just aren't enough hours in the day.

4. How might you overcome some of these barriers as you proceed through the rest of your time in bootcamp?

I have realized that the most important knowledge I can get out of this course right now is the big picture: understanding how a website is structured and how all the pieces fit together. Becoming an expert coder isn't going to happen overnight, it is a lifelong process. In addtion, I have also realised how powerful the LLMs are. I refused to use it at the begining of the course, and gradully using it as my personal tutor.

Reflection on Wellbeing Plan

1. Are any of the walls of my whare in need of strengthening? How might I do this?

Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing): This is the area I really need to strengthen. I've started trimming trees and cleaning up the garden lately. Doing those repetitive physical chores lets me completely switch off my brain, which helps me relax, destress, and get away from the screen.

2. What does my stress profile look like? What do you need when you notice you are stressed?

I got stressed when I lack of time, deadline crashes in work and study, physical exhaustion at the end of the day. I lost patience to others and I look messy. I feel overwhelmed, tired, worried, hard to get focus. I need to stay away from the screen entirely and doing something physical, like triming trees. I pushed my homework due date by a few days after checked with facilitator. I took a day or two off at work. I lowered my learning goal.

3.What is one small, manageable change you can try over the next few weeks?

Look out at the blue sky after every hour of screen time.
