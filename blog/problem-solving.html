1. Were blocked on a simple problem:

What was the problem? I used console.log() for manual testing to check what result I got. However, I was stuck on the Kata gradebook addScore challenge because the test kept failing.

What problem-solving techniques did you use? I talked through the problem (rubber duck) and realized that I forgot to delete the console.log() statement at the end of my code. The manual testing (console.log) was already added the score to Susan[4] , however, the auto-testing code added the score again to Susan[5], causing the test to fail.

How did you feel throughout the process? I felt frustrated at first because everything in the code seemed to work, but I still could not pass the test. Even though the manual testing I added did not change the actual results, it was surprising to see how a small detail like a console.log() statement could cause auto-testing failed.

what did you learn? I learned that testing code can provide useful information about where an error is happening. I also learned that leaving manual testing code in place can interfere with automated tests.

4. Reflect on a time you were reluctant to ask for help. Consider what made you reluctant to do so. What might you try differently next time?

It often depends on the situations, whether I am working independently or as part of a team, and whether I have an urgent deadline to meet. I am usually reluctant to ask for help when I am working independently or I’m learning something new because I feel I need to fully understand it on my own. I worry that asking too early might make me seem unprepared.

However, I’ve realized that trying to solve everything by myself can slow down my progress and sometimes even create more confusion, espeically as a part of team and working toward a same goal. So I plan to ask help strategically by identifying exactly what I don’t understand and then ask.
